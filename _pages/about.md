I'm a second-year PhD student at the [McCombs School of Business](https://www.mccombs.utexas.edu/) Information, Risk, and Operations Management (IROM) Department. I'm fortunate to be advised by [Maria De-Arteaga](https://mariadearteaga.com/) and [Maytal Saar-Tsechansky](http://www.maytals.com/).

New private investment in AI [reached $93.5 billion US dollars](https://aiindex.stanford.edu/wp-content/uploads/2022/03/2022-AI-Index-Report_Master.pdf) in 2021. Despite this huge investment, there is relatively little understanding about whether people **trust** AI to augment their decision making.

<u> My research is focused on understanding the factors that influence <strong>trust</strong> in AI, as well as interventions designed to improve <strong>warranted trust</strong> in systems</u> (i.e. trust the prediction when it is likely accurate, and mistrust when it is not). To this end, I conduct behavioral, ethical, and computational investigations of AI systems, largely across two domains - medicine and fact-checking.

In particular, I'm currently researching: (1) [frameworks for ethical AI in automated misinformation detection](https://dl.acm.org/doi/abs/10.1145/3531146.3533205); (2) how perceptions of data quality used to train and evaluate an AI model affect AI trustworthiness in radiology.
